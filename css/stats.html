Article 4 - Internet Stats (Middle East & Lebanon)

Adoption, Usage, Speed | Last updated: October 13, 2025

Digital Connectivity in the Middle East and Lebanon

"The Internet is becoming the town square for the global village of tomorrow", Bill Gates.

Introduction

In the Middle East, internet adoption varies, but digital adoption is generally high, especially in countries like UAE and Qatar. Lebanon, while having a high percentage of its population using the internet (over 80% in 2023), people struggle with slow and expensive internet services, with average fixed broadband speeds below 10Mbps and a reliance on slower, more costly mobile data. [4.1]

To understand the digital landscape, it is useful to define some key terms:

  • Internet penetration is the percentage of a country’s population that uses internet.
  • Fixed broadband is the high-speed internet that is delivered through fixed lines (for e.g.: DSL, fiber, cable).
  • Mobile Internet is the internet accessed via cellular networks (3G, 4G, 5G).

Internet and Mobile Adoption in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA)

According to GSMA Intelligence, at the end of 2023, around 427 million people in MENA subscribed to a mobile service, representing about 64% of the population. Of those, around 49% were using mobile internet, which represents nearly 327 million people. Mobile internet use in the region has tripled over the past 10 years [4.2].

On the fixed broadband side, while adoption is growing, many countries still lag behind due to infrastructure, costs, and policy challenges. The broadband index report for the MEA area shows that 4G is dominant and that 5G adoption is rising, but unevenly (5G is limited, less than 1% of users have access to 5G technology) [4.3], [4.1].

Lebanon’s Recent figures on Internet Usage and Connectivity

At the start of 2024, there were 4.76 million internet users in Lebanon. Internet penetration was approximately 90.1% of the population based on [4.4]. By early 2025, this rose up to 5.34 million users, with penetration at 91.6% as stated in [4.5]. The number of active cellular mobile connections was around 4.68 million in early 2025, equivalent to 80.4% of the population.

Lebanon’s broadband speed ranking is relatively low globally. In 2024, Lebanon was ranked 196th worldwide with a mean broadband download speed of 9.67 Mbps [4.6]. The mobile internet median download speed is about 30.20 Mbps in January 2025, and the fixed broadband median download speed is approximately 18.78 Mbps in early 2025 [4.7].

Comparative Trends and Challenges

Lebanon’s level of internet penetration is high compared to many countries in the MENA region, but surpassing many literacy and infrastructure hurdles. Fixed broadband speeds are modest, and local content hosting remains limited, increasing latency and dependency on international infrastructure. The growth rate in mobile connections has slowed slightly while broadband speeds, especially fixed broadband, have improved (approximately 36% year-over-year in median fixed speeds) [4.5], [4.6].

In the MENA region overall, while mobile internet adoption is strong, a significant usage gap remains. Many people are covered by networks but do not subscribe or meaningfully use mobile internet due to cost, device availability, or digital literacy [4.2], [4.7].

So, in summary, the Lebanese people are facing the following challenges: outdated infrastructure, high costs, low local content hosted, and security due to a low score in cybersecurity and potential vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Lebanon has achieved high levels of internet adoption, with over 90% of people using the internet, and steady growth in users. Mobile internet speeds are relatively good, and fixed broadband speeds are improving, but still lag behind developed countries. Key challenges include improving local hosting infrastructure, expanding fixed broadband performance more uniformly, and closing access gaps (cost, device availability).
